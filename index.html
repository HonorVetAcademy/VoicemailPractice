BEST PRACTICES

What Makes a Great Voicemail?

US candidates receive dozens of calls. A crisp, confident voicemail makes you memorable and gets callbacks.

⚡

Keep It Under 30 Seconds

Anything longer gets skipped. Aim for 20–28 seconds. Respect the candidate's time — it signals professionalism.

CRITICAL
🎯

State Your Purpose Early

Within the first 5 seconds say who you are and why you're calling. Don't make them guess — they won't call back.

STRUCTURE
📞

Repeat Your Number Slowly

Say your callback number twice — once in the middle, once at the end. Speak each digit clearly. US candidates hate replaying messages.

PRO TIP
🗣️

Smile While You Speak

Your tone comes through in audio. Smiling while recording makes you sound warm, approachable, and energetic — not robotic.

DELIVERY
🪝

One Specific Hook

Mention ONE compelling thing — the pay rate, location, or a unique perk. Vague messages get ignored; specific ones get curiosity.

ENGAGEMENT
🔚

Clear Call to Action

End with exactly what you want them to do: "Please call me back at…" or "I'll try you again tomorrow." Give them a next step.

SELF-REVIEW CHECKLIST

After Each Take, Ask Yourself:

✓
Did I introduce myself clearly?

Name + company within the first 3 seconds.

✓
Did I state the purpose immediately?

The candidate should know why you're calling within 5 seconds.

✓
Did I mention one specific hook?

Pay rate, location, or unique benefit — not everything at once.

✓
Did I give my callback number clearly?

Said slowly, twice if possible. US candidates don't replay messages.

✓
Did I finish under 30 seconds?

Anything longer risks the candidate hanging up before your number.

✓
Was my tone warm and confident?

Not scripted. Not rushed. Friendly, clear, and human.
